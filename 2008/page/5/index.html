Accommodations in Lille &amp; surroundings

Though Jacques has lived in Lille for 23 years, he doesn’t have a clue which hotel is good, and which is not (he’s never had the need to know). But, he does know that for those who’d like to take the time to visit Lille and don’t want to rent a car, the best thing to do is to get a hotel in Lille itself. As such, Jacques’ parents are recommending “La Treille Hotel”. It’s in a great location – 10 minutes away from most of Lille’s historical, cultural and shopping centers. AND, it’s only 2 minutes away from a bus-stop that goes straight to Wambrechies (which by the way, is where the wedding reception is being held). ...

Wedding Location - Lille & Wambrechies

Lille Lille is located in the North of France, near Belgium; it is the 4th largest metropolitan area in France with more than 1 million inhabitants. The city has a strong industrial background, but, after many difficult years, it is now known throughout France for its handsome city center, very active student life and its very active cultural life (European Capital of Culture 2004). How to get there Lille is an important crossroads in the European network. It has frequent (hourly or bi-hourly) TGV connections to Paris (1hour, 51.30 € ), as well as Eurostar connections to Brussels (35min, from 24.60 €) and London (1h45min, from 202.50 €). ...
